# Categories and subcategories

Categories help customers browse the shop. Instead of scrolling through every product, they can filter by category (e.g., "Home Decor", "Toys", "Cable Management").

## How categories work

The structure is two levels deep:

```
Category: Home Decor
  └── Subcategory: Vases
  └── Subcategory: Planters
  └── Subcategory: Wall Art

Category: Toys & Games
  └── Subcategory: Figurines
  └── Subcategory: Puzzles
```

Products are assigned to a subcategory. On the storefront, customers filter by the top-level category.

## Creating a category

1. Go to **Categories** in the sidebar.
2. In the **Add category** form, type the category name.
3. Click **Add**.

The new category appears in the tree below.

## Creating a subcategory

1. Go to **Categories** in the sidebar.
2. In the **Add subcategory** form:
   - Choose the parent category from the dropdown.
   - Type the subcategory name.
3. Click **Add**.

## Assigning a product to a category

When creating or editing a product:

1. Choose a **Category** from the dropdown.
2. Choose a **Subcategory** from the next dropdown (it shows only subcategories that belong to the chosen category).
3. Save the product.

## Deleting a category

You can delete categories and subcategories from the category tree on the Categories page. Categories with products assigned to them cannot be deleted until those products are re-assigned or deleted.

**Tip:** Start with broad category names. You can always add subcategories later. Three to five top-level categories is usually enough for a small store — more than that and the filter starts to feel overwhelming.
