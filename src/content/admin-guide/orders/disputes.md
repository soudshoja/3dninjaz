# Disputes — when customers escalate

A dispute is opened when a customer raises a problem directly with PayPal — for example, they claim they didn't receive the parcel, or the item was significantly different from the description.

Disputes are serious. If you don't respond, PayPal may automatically rule in the customer's favour and refund them.

## Where to see disputes

Go to **Disputes** in the sidebar. Disputes are synced from PayPal automatically (every 15 minutes). You can also click **Refresh** to force an immediate sync.

## Dispute statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Open | Dispute is active — you need to act |
| Waiting for buyer response | You've responded; waiting for the customer |
| Waiting for seller response | PayPal is waiting for you to respond |
| Under review | PayPal is reviewing the evidence |
| Resolved | Dispute is closed |

## How to respond to a dispute

1. Open the dispute from the **Disputes** page.
2. Read the reason and the customer's message.
3. Choose an action:
   - **Accept claim** — agree to refund the customer (PayPal processes it automatically)
   - **Escalate to PayPal** — ask PayPal to mediate (use this if you believe the customer's claim is wrong)
   - **Provide evidence** — upload tracking info, photos, or a description to support your case

## Providing evidence

Good evidence includes:
- Courier tracking screenshot showing "Delivered"
- A photo of the packaged item before shipping
- Any WhatsApp/email messages where the customer confirmed the product was as described

Upload files using the evidence uploader in the dispute detail. Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG.

## Response time

PayPal typically gives sellers **10 days** to respond to a dispute before it's automatically decided in the buyer's favour. Respond as quickly as possible.

## Tips for avoiding disputes

- Always book with a tracked courier (so you have proof of delivery)
- Photograph products before shipping
- Respond promptly to customer WhatsApp/email queries — customers who feel ignored escalate to PayPal
